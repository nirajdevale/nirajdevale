Philosophy

Platform Thinking

Governing the Machine: Human Logic as the Foundation, AI as the Accelerator.

My approach to automation or AI integration always starts with understanding the operational workflow — not the technology. Before introducing automation, I map the process end-to-end, identify bottlenecks, define ownership across operations, engineering, data, and compliance teams, and determine where human judgment is essential. Together, we establish business rules, validation thresholds, reconciliation logic, exception definitions, governance boundaries, and measurable success criteria. Only after the operational foundation is documented, reviewed, and agreed upon do I determine where automation or AI genuinely adds value — and where human oversight must remain.

Once the foundation is established, AI becomes an execution layer rather than the decision-maker. Every automated workflow is designed with clear guardrails, validation logic, and continuous testing to ensure reliability. This approach has been applied while supporting clients like Empower, JP Morgan, and Lincoln Financial, where automation was introduced for reporting, compliance checks, and operational alerts. An AI-powered operations assistant (OPS GPT) was layered on top to retrieve operational data, investigate anomalies, summarize issues, and support quality assurance — reducing investigation turnaround from hours to minutes while keeping business decisions and client communication firmly under human ownership.

Human Layer — What I Own
Map operational workflows and identify bottlenecks, dependencies, and failure points
Define business rules, validation thresholds, reconciliation logic, and governance standards
Author user stories, acceptance criteria, and testing scenarios
Perform reconciliation reviews, quality assurance, and exception resolution
Own stakeholder communication, operational decisions, and continuous process improvements
AI Layer — What AI Executes
Retrieve operational data using predefined database queries
Detect anomalies, reconciliation breaks, and missing or inconsistent data
Validate reports against human-defined business rules and thresholds
Summarize operational issues and assist investigation during quality assurance
Generate alerts, support analyst self-service, and accelerate issue triage at scale
